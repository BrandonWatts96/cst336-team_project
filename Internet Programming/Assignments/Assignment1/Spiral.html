First developed by Barry Bohem in 1986, the spiral method is a process that involves a series of cycles. It was designed with the intent of being a combination of the waterfall and rapid prototyping methodologies with the intent of working both top-down and bottom-up.

Advantages:
Works well with larger and more complex projects as it covers a wider span than supported by more linear methods.
Allows for development of code with the ability to evolve over time more easily.

Disadvantages:
It is possible to fall into "hazardous spiral look-alikes that focus too much on initial development of code.
