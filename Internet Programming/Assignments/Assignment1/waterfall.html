A traditional approach to the process of software design, the waterfall method is a linear method designed with the intent of completing specific tasks and moving on.

Advantages:
In smaller projects, the waterfall method is capable of quickly and efficiently outputting results because of the quick and to-the-point nature of the phases.

Disadvantages:
It is a linear method that discourages revisiting completed phases, making large projects more difficult to complete or touch up on weaker parts.
